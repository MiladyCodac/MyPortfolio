Who am I?

My name is Naomi Marshall, however I prefer Michelle. I grew up on a 64-acre farm in Hoke County near Raeford. I am very proud to know firsthand where our foods come from, and more importantly how to take care of myself if I ever needed to in the future. I love playing the piano, and my favorite book is Kindred by Octavia E. Butler.

What have I done so far?

My main goal when I came to Sandhills was to see what kinds of things I could accomplish. So, while pursuing a double major in Digital Media Technology and Visual Arts, I also tried to push myself to get involved with the community here. I joined the Effective Communication Association, and under the guidance of Professor Kruska, and with the help of my clubmates, I quickly learned that I loved taking on projects in the community. Currently, I am Vice President of the Student Goverment Association as well as Student Ambassador of the Black Lives Matter Committee here on Campus.

Where am I going?

This Fall 2021, I will work on campus, complete 5 classes towards my Digital Media Technology Degree, work as a member of the Hunt Institute/John M. Belk Fellowship, perform my duties as Vice President of SGA and Student Ambassador of the Black Lives Matter Committee, and hopefully I will also be accepted into Phi Theta Kappa. To me, accepting this position of leadership is simply taking responsibility for what I know I can achieve, and that is what I plan to do while I am here!
